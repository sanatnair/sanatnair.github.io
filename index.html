What: Designed and built an RC race car with a 259 mm wheelbase, rear-wheel-drive (RWD) powertrain, and optimized Ackermann steering geometry.

How: Modeled chassis, drivetrain, and suspension in SolidWorks; fabricated components using laser-cut acrylic and 3D-printed parts. Results: Achieved 20–26 mph top speed with ~2 s 0–10 mph acceleration; completed 8 successful track laps with ~7 s best lap time. Demonstrated durability and stable handling throughout race tests.

Texas Guadaloop — Hyperloop Pod Frame & Thermal Systems

Structures & Manufacturing Team — Mechanical Design & Simulation

Product Design CAD/FEA Thermal Systems Composites

What: Designed structural and thermal subsystems for the Texas Guadaloop Hyperloop pod, including the composite chassis and propulsion cooling architecture.

How: Created a manufacturable pod frame in SolidWorks; conducted static/dynamic FEA and vibration analysis to meet strength-to-weight targets under acceleration loads. Modeled a phase-change-material-based cooling system and performed thermal simulations to ensure motor reliability. Supported fabrication via welding and composite layups.

Results: Delivered a lightweight, robust chassis meeting structural safety factors (> 2); integrated propulsion, electronics, and levitation subsystems onto the completed frame.
